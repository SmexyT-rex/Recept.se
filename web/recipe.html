Slow-Roasted Balsamic Glazed Salmon

A masterful balance of sharp Modena reduction and the delicate, buttery profile of king salmon, prepared with meticulous thermal precision.

person Author ID: 9422
calendar_today Created: Oct 24, 2024

01 Ingredients

  • King Salmon Fillet

    2 lbs

  • Aged Balsamic Vinegar

    1/2 cup

  • Wildflower Honey

    2 tbsp

  • Fresh Garlic

    3 cloves

  • Rosemary & Thyme

    4 sprigs

  • Maldon Sea Salt

    1 pinch

02 Preparation

Step One

Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C). Pat the salmon dry with paper towels—moisture is the enemy of a perfect glaze adhesion.

Step Two

In a small saucepan, combine balsamic, honey, and garlic. Simmer over low heat until reduced by half. It should coat the back of a spoon with a heavy, syrupy consistency.

Step Three

Place salmon in a cast-iron skillet skin-side down. Brush generously with the reduction. Nestle herbs around the fish. Roast for 22-25 minutes, basting every 8 minutes.

Step Four

Remove from oven and let rest for 4 minutes. Brush one final time with any remaining glaze before serving with a sprinkle of Maldon salt.
